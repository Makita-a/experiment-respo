WORK & RESEARCH

Cybersecurity Research · 2024 – Present
SAKO LAB · WASEDA
AI-Based Smart Contract Vulnerability Detection
Visit Lab ↗

I am a member of the Sako Laboratory — a cybersecurity research group at Waseda University — where my work sits at the intersection of Artificial Intelligence and blockchain security.

My research focuses on using machine learning to automatically detect vulnerabilities in smart contracts. In particular, I work on identifying reentrancy attacks — one of the most notorious vulnerability classes in smart contract history, responsible for some of the largest hacks in DeFi, including The DAO hack (2016) where $60 million was drained.

My broader interests include cybersecurity, blockchain security, software engineering, AI, and secure system design.

Student Club · Waseda University
GDSC · WASEDA
Google Developer Student Clubs — Frontend Team

I was a member of the Google Developer Student Club (GDSC) at Waseda University, where I worked on the frontend team.

GDSC is a Google-supported program that brings together students passionate about technology, development, and community. At Waseda, we collaborated on real projects, ran workshops, and helped bridge the gap between student developers and industry tools.

Being on the frontend team meant designing and building interfaces — a natural extension of my interest in art, design, and how things look and feel for real users.
