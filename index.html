Cybersecurity Research · 2024 – Present
AI-BASED SMART CONTRACT
VULNERABILITY DETECTION

I am a member of the Sako Laboratory — a cybersecurity research group at Waseda University — where my work sits at the intersection of Artificial Intelligence and blockchain security.

My research focuses on using machine learning to automatically detect vulnerabilities in smart contracts self-executing programs running on blockchain networks. In particular, I work on identifying reentrancy attacks, one of the most notorious vulnerability classes in smart contract history.

A reentrancy attack lets a malicious contract repeatedly call back into the victim before the first call finishes like draining an ATM in a loop before the bank notices. The infamous DAO hack (2016) exploited exactly this, draining $60 million.

My goal is to build tools that catch these patterns automatically, before a contract goes live.

GDSC · WASEDA

Frontend Member

I was a member of the Google Developer Student Club (GDSC) at Waseda University, where I worked on the frontend team.

GDSC is a Google-supported program that brings together students passionate about technology, development, and community. At Waseda, we collaborated on real projects, ran workshops, and helped bridge the gap between student developers and industry tools.

Being on the frontend team meant designing and building interfaces, a natural place for someone who loves both code and design. It pushed me to think about how things look and feel for real users, not just how they work under the hood.

It was also where I got to meet a lot of driven, curious people from across the university, which, honestly, was just as valuable as the technical work.
