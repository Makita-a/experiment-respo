Research — Sako Lab
Cybersecurity Laboratory · Waseda University
AI-Based Smart Contract Vulnerability Detection

I am currently a member of the Sako Laboratory, a cybersecurity research group at Waseda University.


My research focuses on applying Artificial Intelligence to detect vulnerabilities in blockchain smart contracts. In particular, I work on identifying security issues such as reentrancy attacks — a class of vulnerability where a malicious contract repeatedly calls back into the victim contract before the first execution is complete, potentially draining funds.


I explore how machine learning techniques can improve automated vulnerability detection and security analysis of smart contract bytecode and source code. My broader interests include cybersecurity, blockchain security, software engineering, AI, and secure system design.
